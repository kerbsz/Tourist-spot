Tourist Spot in Pagudpud

  1. Saud Beach
  2. Cape Bojeador Lighthouse
  3. Patapat Viaduct

  5. Blue Lagoon
  6. Bangui Windmills

I. Saud Beach
Saud Beach in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, is a well-known and admired beach in the Philippines, having been named one of the "25 Most Beautiful Beaches in the World." Despite its reputation, it is not marketed and is frequently uncrowded, with tall coconut trees giving shade and crystal-clear water.

II. Cape Bojeador Lighthouse
Cape Bojeador Lighthouse, also known as Burgos Lighthouse, is a man-made attraction in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte. First lit in 1898, it guides ships for over 100 years. Known for its intact architecture, it was named a National Historic Landmark in 2004 and a National Cultural Treasure in 2011.

III. Patapat-viaduct
Patapat Viaduct is a 1.3-kilometer-long concrete bridge in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It is the country's fourth-longest bridge and serves as an extension of the Maharlika Highway. The bridge, located at the foot of the Northern Cordillera Mountain Ranges, provides breathtaking views of the West Philippine Sea, Pasaleng Bay, and surrounding landscapes, making it a well-known landmark in Pagudpud.

IV. Bantay Abot Cave
Bantay Abot Cave, a unique arching rock formation, translates to "a mountain with a hole" and resembles a half donut from a distance. Known as the female half of Lover's Rock, it's a popular stop on Pagudpud trips due to its distinctive shape and position facing the West Philippine Sea.

V. Blue Lagoon
Blue Lagoon Beach, originally Maira-Ira, is a popular Pagudpud beach in Ilocos Norte, but many people refer to it as Blue Lagoon Pagudpud because of its crystal blue wate compared to Saud beach. Developed and with more tourist activities, it features restaurants, huts, homestays, and resorts. Activities include surfing, banana boat riding, and ziplining are also available.
